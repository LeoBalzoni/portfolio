I've been building software for a while now, but I've never really written about it publicly. That changes today.

## Why now?

A few reasons. First, I've noticed that the engineers I admire most are the ones who share their thinking — not just their code. Writing forces you to clarify half-baked ideas, and half-baked ideas are where most of the interesting work happens.

Second, I want a place to document what I'm learning. The best debugging session you'll ever have is the one where you explain the problem to someone else. A blog post is that, at scale.

## What to expect

I'll write about:

- **Architecture decisions** — the tradeoffs, not just the final answer
- **Tools and workflows** — what I actually use day-to-day and why
- **Lessons learned** — things I wish I'd known earlier
- **Side projects** — building things in public

I'm not trying to write definitive guides. I'm trying to think out loud. If something resonates or you disagree, reach out — that's the whole point.

## The stack

This site is built with Next.js, Tailwind, shadcn/ui, and Framer Motion, deployed to GitHub Pages. Blog posts are MDX files — markdown with the ability to embed React components when I need something interactive.

I built most of it with Claude, which was a great exercise in describing what you want clearly enough for an AI to produce it. More on that in a future post.

Let's go.
